The Problem

Life doesn't wait for you to figure it out. The prenuptial conversation happens before you're ready. The first household budget is built on guesswork. The parental leave plan is written at midnight during the third trimester. The career negotiation happens before you've ever practiced one.

For every major transition — every chapter of your life — there are decisions to make, systems to build, and a path to navigate. And almost none of us have a guide.

Chapters is that guide. Not advice. Not a quiz. A platform — one that understands the chapter you're in, gives you the tools to navigate it, and stays with you as your life moves forward.

Why Now · The Urgency Case
The category is unbuilt.
The window closes once.
01
No direct competitor exists today
There is no unified adult lifecycle platform from Intuit, LegalZoom, or any funded startup. The category is completely unbuilt. This is the rarest condition in consumer software — large TAM, zero competition. It will not last more than 3–5 years once this document exists in the world.
02
AI makes this buildable by one person
Three years ago this required 50 engineers and $20M. Today: one founder, Cursor, Claude, AWS, and Stripe. First movers can build at a speed incumbents cannot match. The company can be live with paying users before a competitor can staff their first team.
03
72 million millennials are peak user age now
Ages 28–43. Getting engaged, buying homes, having children, building wealth — right now. This demographic peak does not repeat. The platform that captures them now retains them for 20 years of compounding revenue across all three libraries.
04
BookTok is a ready distribution channel
200 billion views. The Chapters brand speaks the language of that community natively. Founder's fiancée is an active creator in this space. Organic distribution before a single dollar of paid marketing — to the exact demographic living these life moments today.
05
Post-COVID: digital trust for life events is permanent
Pre-2020, consumers were skeptical of digital tools for high-stakes decisions. Post-COVID, the same consumers sign mortgages, file taxes, open investment accounts, and attend therapy entirely online. The behavioral barrier to digital life planning has been permanently removed.
06
The founder is the user — right now
Thomas O'Leary is personally navigating a prenuptial agreement (October 2026 wedding), planning the wedding, and managing a slip-and-fall legal case simultaneously. He is living inside three of the most important modules at this exact moment. The product authenticity of a founder who is the user in real time is not replicable.
